Phase 1 — Front Cam + Full Backend
Power · Compute · 4G · Front camera · First boot
1
Power Entry — Fusebox Tap
Scirocco fusebox is on the driver’s right (RHD). Fit two Add-a-Circuit taps: one constant 12V (rear demist slot, ~20A) and one ACC/ignition fuse (radio slot, ~10A). Run 22AWG red left across the underside of the dash. The chassis ground bolt is a bare M8 stub under the right dash — sand clean before connecting.
12V Constant ACC Sense Chassis GND
2
ESP32-C3 — Ignition Brain
The ESP32-C3 SuperMini monitors the ACC wire. Ignition off = ACC drops to 0V = ESP32 fires a UART safe-shutdown to the Pi over TX/RX. Pi gets ~30s to flush its buffer and power down before UPS cuts. Flash the shutdown firmware onto the ESP32 before installing.
ACC → ESP32 UART → Pi UPS relay control
3
UPS HAT + LiFePO4 Battery
The Waveshare UPS HAT takes 12V from the constant fuse, steps down to clean 5V/5A for the Pi, and bridges to the LiFePO4 battery for park-mode power. Mount on 3M Dual Lock in the glovebox void. Leave ventilation space. In a sudden power cut the UPS bridges to battery — Pi never drops.
12V → 5V LiFePO4 bridge Park mode
4
Brain Install — Glovebox Void
Remove glovebox (two Torx inside, two spring clips top, unplug light). Mount Pi CM5 IO Board on 3M Dual Lock on the ceiling of the void. The NVMe plugs into the M.2 PCIe slot. The USB hub into the USB-C host port — all cameras connect through this.
PCIe x1 NVMe USB 3.2 hub UART shutdown
5
4G — Roof Antenna to Glovebox
Shark-fin antenna through the roof (use existing grommet or drill 20mm). Route SMA coax down the passenger A-pillar trim into the glovebox. Quectel EC25-E plugs into the hub. Insert Smarty SIM — Tailscale auto-connects to Hetzner relay on boot.
SMA coax RF USB to hub 4G → Tailscale
6
Front Camera — Mirror to Pi CSI
IMX678 in a 3D-printed housing behind the rearview mirror, lens forward. FPC ribbon goes LEFT (passenger side) — never the curtain airbag side. Down the passenger A-pillar trim, into the glovebox, to Pi CSI port 0. Loop the ribbon once before the connector to avoid stress on the flex.
MIPI FPC ribbon Passenger A-pillar ⚠ Away from airbag
7
✅ Phase 1 Complete — First Boot
Ignition on: ACC → ESP32 → UPS enables 5V → Pi boots (~30s) → GStreamer records front cam. Access dashboard via Tailscale IP on your phone. Run df -h — confirm NVMe mounted and growing. Ignition off — watch clean UART shutdown (~35s, then UPS cuts).
✓ System proven Recording + remote access live

Longer run. IMX307 driver cam cable goes under right sill, under rear bench trim, across to left sill, forward to hub. Critical: leave a 10cm service loop at the B-pillar — door sill flex will shear an unlooped cable over time. Always use shielded cable here.

CVBS fisheye cam at the plate, powered from reverse light positive. 4-core cable (composite + 12V + GND) up through boot sill, up C-pillar, to the CVBS switcher in the glovebox.

Prometheus

Stealing fire from the fusebox — safely extracting power without cutting factory wires

1. Access Driver-Side Fusebox
2. Piggyback "Add-A-Circuit"
3. Earth to Bare Metal Bolt

1 Find the Empty / Safe Slots

  • Open the driver's side door and pry off the plastic end-panel of the dashboard to reveal the fusebox.
  • Use a multimeter to find a Constant 12V fuse (usually a 12V socket or interior lights).
  • Find an ACC 12V fuse (one that ONLY turns on when the engine is running, like the radio or wipers).

2 Install the Piggyback Fuses

  • Remove the original car fuse and plug it into the BOTTOM slot of the Piggyback tap.
  • Plug a new 5A fuse into the TOP slot of the tap (this protects the dashcam).
  • Plug the entire tap back into the car's fuse slot. Ensure the wire points DOWN.

3 Route the Master Power Lines

  • Crimp the thick RED wire to the piggyback tap's blue connector.
  • Bolt the thick BLACK ground wire to a bare metal chassis bolt deep under the steering wheel.
  • Zip-tie the red and black wires together and run them behind the radio console over to the passenger glovebox opening.

The Winding Staircase

1 Disassemble Glovebox

  • Remove the 6x Torx T20 screws securing the VW Scirocco glovebox.
  • Carefully unplug the passenger airbag switch and the glovebox light.
  • Drop the unit out to expose the massive void behind the dashboard.

2 Mount the Brain

  • Take the 3D-printed Spectre Brain Box (containing Pi, NVMe, ESP32, and Battery).
  • Apply strips of 3M Dual Lock (Industrial Velcro) to the top of the box.
  • Press firmly against the flat plastic ceiling of the glovebox cavity.

3 CAUTION: Route Front Camera

  • Run the flat MIPI ribbon cable up the driver-side A-pillar.
  • CRITICAL: Ensure the ribbon runs BEHIND the curtain airbag, not in front of it. Tape it to the existing factory loom.
  • Tuck across the front edge of the headliner to the rearview mirror.

4 Route Side Cameras

  • Run the thick, shielded USB-C cables down to the driver and passenger side sill plates (under the carpet edge).
  • Run them back to the B-Pillars, then straight up the pillar trim.
  • Leave 10cm of slack at the top to plug into the 3D-printed IMX307 stealth housings.
